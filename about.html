About Me

Hello! I am Amin Mojiri, an enthusiastic and dedicated Environmental Engineer with over 10 years of experience in the field. I specialize in water and wastewater treatment, the fate and removal of emerging contaminants such as PFAS and PPCPs, and atmospheric water harvesting.

Currently, I am an Assistant Research Professor at Arizona State University, where I focus on leveraging data analytics, machine learning, and generative AI to develop innovative environmental solutions. My academic journey includes a PhD in Environmental Engineering from the University of Science Malaysia, and I am further enhancing my skills with a MS in Artificial Intelligence from European University Cyprus and an MBA in Business Analytics from O.P. Jindal Global University, India.

Throughout my career, I have had the opportunity to serve in various academic and research positions across the globe, including as an Assistant Professor at Hiroshima University, Japan, and a Visiting Researcher at University Technology MARA, Malaysia. My work has been recognized with several awards, and I am a proactive contributor to numerous scientific publications and a reviewer for esteemed journals.

In addition to my technical skills, I am proficient in multiple languages and have a strong track record in effectively collaborating with diverse international teams. I am deeply committed to the advancement of sustainable and innovative environmental practices.

Feel free to explore more about my projects and academic contributions on this site or contact me directly for collaboration or consultation opportunities.
